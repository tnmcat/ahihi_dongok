    Kanchenjunga Trek Overview

    Those who long to travel a isolated part of the Himalaya are in for a rare treat as this journey begins in the remote state of Sikkim. The region was closed to foreigners for most of its existence and offers a chance to travel though an unspoiled Buddhist area as you make your way to the world 3rd highest mountain. While most treks to ‘Kanch’ take place on the Nepali side, the India side trek is far off the beaten path, as we wind our way through farming villages eventually leading to the big mountains with views of Everest and Kanchenjunga.

    KANCHENJUNGA TREK HIGHLIGHTS

    Trek in remote area of India to the base camp of Kanchenjunga the world’s 3rd highest mountain.
    Remote trek that sees very few tourists.
    Travels through an ancient Buddhist culture.
    15 days on trail.
    Guided by Alpine Ascents India Expert Yatish Bahuguna .

    The trip is physically demanding but requires no technical experience. Porters and pack animals carry the majority of your gear. The trek is steep in parts but we generally travel unhurried. Pack weight is around 15 pounds.

    Darjeeling Darjeeling, Mount Kanchenjunga can be seen from Observatory Hill but a much better view of the peak is from Tiger Hill , 10 km south of Darjeeling . On the way back, you can stop at Ghoom, a small Tibetan monastery by the roadside, built in 1875 and housing a 5- meter statue of Lord Buddha .
